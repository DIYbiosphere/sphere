MANILA BIOPUNK

A small intellectual and cultural movement composed of young science and engineering professionals, students, advocates, hobbyists and enthusiasts who are organizing to create public awareness about the current technologies in the biological and chemical sciences which can be applied in one's own backyard or garage.

The group members are dedicated to making biology an accessible pursuit for every Filipino who wants to be involved in scientific research and development even if they do not have enough background. The group also aims to educate Filipinos about the basic principles of biotechnology, bioethics and biosafety.

Feel free to post protocols of any kind of experiment. Ideally materials are cheap and/or are readily accessible in backyards, kitchen, junk shops, supermarkets, etc., but otherwise we can try raising funds for bigger experiments too. Experiments can be plant or animal tissue culture, microbial culture (nonpathogenic), beer brewing, cell component extraction, organic synthesis, biology and art, and many more!

EMOTIVE LANGUAGE IN SCIENCE
A campaign by those from Sense About Science to make lay people become aware of how emotive language affect their judgement and bias when it comes to topics on science, making it hard to distinguish from pseudoscience.
We're not asking everyone to start being really serious about organic chemistry and take a course (although that would be nice too!). We're just asking everyone to chill and get familiar with what it really means when people use "chemical", "toxic", "natural", etc. to describe a substance. Most of the time, its not what you think. Even most doctors have the wrong idea and are victims of pseudoscientific marketing ploys like "detox" diets.
BE AWARE! Know the word before using it :)
